A tribute to Munshi Premchand

Munshi Premchand : Upanyas Samrat (King of Novels)

The Voice of the Common man
Munshi Premchand (31 July 1880 - 8 October 1936)

Timeline

  • 1880: Born in Lamhi, Varanasi, India.
  • 1901: Published his first short story, "Duniya ka Sabse Anmol Ratan"
  • 1915: Published his first novel, "Soz-e-Watan"
  • 1921: Became a prominent figure in Hindi literature with "Godaan"
  • 1936: Passed away, leaving behind a legacy of over 300 short stories, novels, and plays.
  • 1952: The Government of India established the Munshi Premchand Award for excellence in Hindi literature.
  • 1976: The Premchand Park in New Delhi was inaugurated in his memory.
  • 1980: The centenary celebrations of his birth were held across India.
  • 2000: The Premchand Memorial Museum was established in Varanasi.
  • 2010: The Premchand Literary Festival was initiated to celebrate his contributions to literature.
  • 2020: The 140th birth anniversary was celebrated with various literary events and discussions.
  • 2023: The Munshi Premchand International Literary Award was established to honor writers who contribute to the legacy of Premchand.
